Critical Wireless Design Aspects and RF Design Process

Our professional design takes capacity as well as coverage into account. Covering the areas where signal is needed is relatively easy to do and definitely part of the equation.

However, coverage just is not enough. Today, you need context and that means capacity. An optimally designed wireless network takes the different kinds and amount of devices being used as well as the applications being run on those devices into account An experienced hotel wireless solution does not just add more access points and hope for the best.

Word to the wise, just throwing up more access points can actually create more issues and ends up putting a hole on your pockets.

This is why we have pointed out below the most critical and important aspects and methodologies we are following.

Gather Requirements

  • Business Requirements
  • Blue Prints / CAD Drawings
  • Facility Layouts & Construction Materials
  • Client Number and Mix
  • Client Density Distribution
  • Critical Applications / Business Process

Pre - Installation Design

  • Predictive Modeling
  • Wall attenuation measurements
  • “AP on a stick” measurement
  • Wired network integration

Post Installation Validation

  • RF Site Survey (active / passive / spectrum)
  • RF Tuning Channel and Power Plans
  • Association and Authentication Tests
  • Roaming Tests
  • Spectrum Analysis
